<p><span>If you are a global brand leader, you have likely sat in a meeting where someone pulled up a roadmap slide labeled “Omnichannel 202x,” or presented a marketing deck promising a “seamless omnichannel experience.”</span></p> 
<p><span>According to Marco Brucato, Head of eCommerce DTC Transformation at <a href="https://www.bekoeurope.com/">Beko Europe</a>, this moment is a major red flag.</span></p> 
<p>“I hate the word omnichannel,’” Brucato<a href="https://www.linkedin.com/feed/update/urn:li:activity:7414594035115417600/?actorCompanyId=69601935"><span style="text-decoration: none;">declared on LinkedIn</span></a>. “Because if you need to label it, it means that you don’t have it. Omnichannel shouldn’t be a buzzword that you can use one day and the other one not. It should be just part of your corporate strategy.”</p> 
<p><span>In conversation with hosts Rachel Tipographand Sarah Hofstetter on the BRAVE COMMERCE podcast, Brucato </span><a href="https://www.adweek.com/brand-marketing/beko-europes-marco-brucato-on-scaling-ecommerce-without-losing-local-relevance/" style="text-decoration: none;">delivered a masterclass</a><span> on what it actually takes to scale digital commerce across complex global markets while maintaining local relevance. He didn’t offer a three-step hack for better retail media tags. Instead, he challenged the very semantics we use to discuss commerce maturity.</span></p> 
<p><span>Here is why Brucato believes “omnichannel” can be a dangerous word, and what leaders should do to embed it as an operating principle.</span></p>
